# ‚≠ê **What Defined 1980s Synths?**

## **1. The FM Revolution**

Nothing impacted music more than **Yamaha‚Äôs DX7 (1983)**.

* Totally new harmonic world
* Electric pianos, bells, plucks, basses
* Sharp, digital, percussive timbres
* Clean, bright, cutting mix presence
* Mass-market price

FM became **the sound of the decade**, heard in every genre:

* Pop (Whitney Houston)
* R&B (Sade)
* Rock (Phil Collins)
* New Age (Enya, Kitaro)
* Film scoring
* Video games (the YM2612 Genesis sound chip was FM)

FM changed everything.

---

## **2. MIDI Arrives (1983) and Changes The World**

Before MIDI, instruments couldn't talk to each other.

With MIDI:

* One keyboard could control multiple synths
* Sequencing exploded
* Racks of gear became standard
* Computer-assisted music production took off
* Studios standardized workflows

The **Prophet-600** and **Juno-106** were early adopters.
By the late 80s? Every synth had MIDI.

This was the *internet moment* of music gear.

---

## **3. DCO Polysynths Take Over**

DCOs (Digitally Controlled Oscillators) offered:

* Analog tone
* Digital tuning stability
* Lower cost
* Faster manufacturing

This created an explosion of affordable polysynths:

* Roland Juno-6/60/106
* Roland JX-3P / JX-8P
* Korg Poly-800
* Oberheim Matrix-6

The Juno-106 alone appears in tens of thousands of tracks.

---

## **4. Digital Sampling Arrives**

Sampling turned synthesizers into:

* Orchestras
* Drum machines
* Choirs
* Real instruments
* Any sound imaginable

The key instruments:

* **Fairlight CMI (1980‚Äì82)** ‚Äî expensive, elite, groundbreaking
* **E-mu Emulator I/II** ‚Äî the sampler of synthpop
* **Akai S-series + MPC60** ‚Äî the heart of hip-hop
* **E-mu SP-12/SP-1200** ‚Äî golden-age hip-hop‚Äôs drum engine

Sampling changed the *definition* of what a synthesizer could be.

---

## **5. Digital/Analog Hybrid Synthesis**

Some 80s synths used digital oscillators + analog filters:

* **PPG Wave** (wavetable + analog VCF)
* **Korg DW-6000/8000**
* **Roland D-50** (partial PCM, partial synthesis)
* **Fairlight & Emulator II** (sample ‚Üí analog filters)
* **Ensoniq ESQ-1** (digital oscillators + Curtis filters)

This was the first step toward the workstation era.

---

## **6. The Rise of Drum Machines**

Today‚Äôs pop, hip-hop, R&B, house, techno ‚Äî all trace roots here.

Key machines:

* **TR-808** (1982): the heartbeat of hip-hop, electro, R&B
* **TR-909** (1983): the backbone of house and techno
* **LinnDrum** (1982): the sound of 80s pop & rock
* **SP-12** (1986): gritty sampling for hip-hop

The drum machine became a **genre-defining tool**.

---

## **7. The Digital Workstation Is Born**

By the late 80s:

* Massive ROM sample libraries
* Integrated sequencers
* Effects
* Multi-timbral engines
* Performance setups

The **Korg M1 (1988)** became the best-selling synthesizer of all time.

It changed pop music, worship music, film scoring, and more.
