# ‚≠ê **What Defined 1970s Synths?**

## **1. Analog Everything**

All 1970s synths were **true analog**:

* Voltage-controlled oscillators (VCOs)
* Voltage-controlled filters (VCFs)
* Voltage-controlled amplifiers (VCAs)
* ADSR envelope generators
* CV/Gate or S-Trig signaling

The sound was warm, unstable, rich, and very alive.

This era created the ‚Äúanalog warmth‚Äù mythology.

---

## **2. Synths Become Portable (Goodbye, Wall of Modules)**

In the late 60s, synths were giant modular cabinets.
The **Minimoog Model D (1970)** changed everything:

* No patch cables
* Pre-wired signal path
* Pitch/mod wheels
* A hard case
* A price musicians could afford

This made synthesizers instruments you could **tour with**, not just studio science projects.

Without the Minimoog, synths don‚Äôt break into mainstream music.

---

## **3. The Rise of Polyphonic Synths**

Early polysynths were crude ‚Äústring machines.‚Äù
But in the mid‚Äìlate '70s, true polyphonic analog emerged:

* **Oberheim 4-Voice / 8-Voice (SEM)**
* **Moog Polymoog**
* **Korg PS-series (PS-3100 / PS-3300)**
* **Yamaha CS-50 / CS-60 / CS-80**

These keyboards introduced:

* Chords
* Expressive poly-aftertouch
* Multi-layer sounds
* Huge sonic capabilities

Polyphony in the 70s was **a miracle of engineering.**

---

## **4. The Birth of Synth Icons**

Nearly every iconic analog synth lineage **starts in the 1970s**:

* **Moog** (Minimoog, Polymoog)
* **ARP** (Odyssey, 2600, Pro Soloist, Omni)
* **Oberheim** (SEM family ‚Üí OB-X)
* **Korg** (MS-20, PS-series)
* **Roland** (SH-series, System-100, Jupiter-4)
* **Yamaha** (GX-1, CS-Series culminating in CS-80)
* **EMS** (VCS3, Synthi)

Some of these instruments remain **unparalleled** to this day.

## **5. Proto-Digital & Early Sequencing**

While the 1970s were analog-driven, a few digital/conceptual leaps began:

* **Fairlight‚Äôs precursors** were being conceptualized
* Basic analog sequencers (Moog 960, ARP sequencer) arrived

* Kraftwerk pioneered whole electronic albums using analog machinery

This set the stage for the sampling & digital revolutions of the 80s.
