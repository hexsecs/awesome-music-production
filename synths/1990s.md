# ‚≠ê **What Defined 1990s Synths?**

## **1. Virtual Analog Takes Over**

In the mid-90s, companies realized musicians missed the hands-on feel of analog.
Digital DSP improved enough to **simulate analog behavior**, birthing:

* **Clavia Nord Lead (1995)**
* **Roland JP-8000 (1997, Supersaw)**
* **Yamaha AN1x (1997)**
* **Access Virus (1997)**
* **Novation Supernova (1998)**

VA synths gave musicians:

* Knobs again (after a decade of membrane buttons)
* Big unison ‚Äúanalog-style‚Äù leads and pads
* Fast envelopes, real-time performance control
* Perfect tuning + digital reliability

This shaped techno, trance, big beat, IDM, and early EDM.

---

## **2. Workstations Become King**

The workstation became the **default keyboard** of the 1990s.

Leading instruments:

* **Korg Trinity (1995)** ‚Äì Touchscreen revolution.
* **Korg 01/W (1991)** ‚Äì Warm waveshaping digital classic.
* **Yamaha SY99 (1992)** ‚Äì Deep FM + sample-loading hybrid.
* **Roland JV-80 / JV-1080 (1992 / 1994)** ‚Äì The best-selling studio ROMpler module.
* **Kurzweil K2000 (1994)** ‚Äì V.A.S.T. full-modular digital engine.

Workstations were used for:

* Film scores
* TV themes
* Studio sessions
* Pop production
* Worship music
* Live bands

They replaced entire racks of the 1980s.

---

## **3. Sampling Becomes Mature & Powerful**

While the 80s invented sampling, the 90s *perfected* it.

Key instruments:

* **Akai S3000/S5000** series
* **E-mu E4 Ultra**
* **Ensoniq ASR-10** (1992) ‚Äì massive cult following
* **Roland S-770/S-760**

Sampling became:

* Higher fidelity
* Cheaper
* More memory
* Faster editing
* More expressive
* More multitimbral

Hip-hop, R&B, and electronic music exploded because of it.

---

## **4. Physical Modeling Appears**

A radical new synthesis technology debuted:

### **Yamaha VL1 (1994)**

First commercial **physical modeling** synth:

* Breath, reed, lip, bow, tube models
* Ultra-expressive
* Huge in jazz fusion and film scoring

Korg followed with:

### **Korg Z1 (1997)**

A legendary polymorphing synth using MOSS modeling.

These machines were futuristic, expressive, and still unique today.

---

## **5. Wavesequencing & Transwaves Expand Digital Synthesis**

Building on the PPG Wave legacy, the 1990s refined evolving textures:

* **Korg Wavestation (1990)** ‚Äì Wavesequencing ambient giant
* **Ensoniq TS-10 / SD-1 (1991‚Äì93)** ‚Äì Early ‚ÄúTranswaves‚Äù
* **Waldorf Microwave II/XT (1997‚Äì98)** ‚Äì Digital wavetable powerhouses

These instruments shaped ambient, trance, new age, and film soundscapes.

---

## **6. Techno, House, Trance & Big Beat Drive Synth Innovation**

The dance explosion of the 1990s demanded:

* Fast envelopes
* Huge unison layers
* Digital overdrive
* Punchy VA basses
* Otherworldly pads

Artists like The Prodigy, Orbital, The Chemical Brothers, Underworld, Aphex Twin, and Massive Attack **defined a generation of synth use**.

---

## **7. Grooveboxes Arrive**

For the first time, synths + sequencers + drums came together:

* **Roland MC-303 (1996)** ‚Äì the revival of acid culture
* **Roland MC-505 (1998)** ‚Äì full-fledged rave workstation
* **Korg Electribe ER-1 / EA-1 (1999)** ‚Äì affordable analog-modeling Beatboxes
* **Yamaha RM1x (1999)** ‚Äì sequencing powerhouse

Dance and techno became accessible to everyone.
